# Watchkeeping Simulator — 18-Week Progression

**STCW Reference:** OICNW, Table A-II/1
**Primary Competency:** Competency 2 — Maintain a safe navigational watch
**Integrated Competency:** Competency 7 — Use English in written and oral form (SMCP)
**Supporting Competencies:** 3 (Radar/ARPA), 5 (Emergencies), 6 (Distress)

---

## Design Philosophy

This is **not** an English course. It is bridge watchkeeping immersion. The cadet learns to *stand a watch* — to read traffic, assess danger, apply COLREGS, and communicate decisions. English is the **operating language of the bridge**, never a separate subject. Every weekly task is delivered, reported, or communicated in maritime English using SMCP.

The semester is built on **real AIS scenarios** (captured from Tampico / Altamira) and live data feeds (AIS traffic, weather, sea state). Difficulty climbs from reading a static traffic picture to standing a full independent watch under pressure.

**Academy rule (from `stcw-competencies.md`):** every weekly task must map to at least one STCW competency. Each week below names its competency anchor.

---

## Structure

Eighteen weeks, five phases. Each week is one task the cadet completes to progress. Phases build on each other — a cadet cannot evaluate collision risk (Phase 2) before reading the picture (Phase 1).

| Phase | Weeks | Focus | STCW Skill |
|-------|-------|-------|------------|
| 1 | 1–4 | Reading the Watch Picture | Look-out, situational awareness |
| 2 | 5–8 | Detecting Danger | Traffic monitoring, collision assessment |
| 3 | 9–12 | The Rules of the Road | Collision avoidance (COLREGS) |
| 4 | 13–16 | Bridge Communication | Reporting procedures (SMCP, VHF, VTS) |
| 5 | 17–18 | The Full Watch | Watch handover, integrated assessment |

# PHASE 1 — Reading the Watch Picture (Weeks 1–4)

*The cadet learns to build situational awareness: what is out there, what state each vessel is in, and what the picture means. Foundation of all watchkeeping (COLREGS Rule 5 — Look-out).*

### Week 1 — Contact Identification
- **STCW skill:** Look-out duties; situational awareness
- **Scenario:** Static Tampico scenario (20 real vessels). Cadet receives the AIS picture.
- **Task:** Classify every contact by navigational status — under way, at anchor, moored, restricted in ability to manoeuvre. Identify vessel types from AIS data.
- **English deliverable:** A written contact list using correct AIS/maritime terminology ("MV CHAMPION CORNELIA, container vessel, at anchor, bearing 045, 1.2 NM").
- **Assessment:** Status codes read correctly; terminology precise.

### Week 2 — Building the Mental Picture
- **STCW skill:** Situational awareness
- **Scenario:** Same scenario, now with relative bearings and ranges from own ship.
- **Task:** Plot the traffic relative to own vessel. Distinguish vessels ahead, on the beam, astern. Identify which contacts are closest.
- **English deliverable:** A spoken (recorded) traffic summary as if briefing the relieving officer — "We have three vessels at anchor in the outer anchorage, one tug under way to the north..."
- **Assessment:** Spatial picture correct; bridge briefing is clear and ordered.

### Week 3 — Movement vs. Stationary
- **STCW skill:** Traffic monitoring (introductory)
- **Scenario:** Two scenarios captured minutes apart (or live feed) so vessels have moved.
- **Task:** Identify which vessels are making way and which are stopped. Read SOG/COG. Flag any data inconsistency (e.g., a "moored" vessel showing speed — a real AIS quality issue worth teaching).
- **English deliverable:** A movement report distinguishing vessels "under way and making way" from those "stopped" or "at anchor."
- **Assessment:** Movement read correctly; cadet questions inconsistent data rather than trusting it blindly.

### Week 4 — The Look-out Report (Phase 1 checkpoint)
- **STCW skill:** Look-out duties (COLREGS Rule 5)
- **Scenario:** Full Tampico picture + simulated visual/weather conditions.
- **Task:** Produce a complete look-out report integrating AIS, visual, and conditions. State what requires attention.
- **English deliverable:** A formal watch look-out report in SMCP style.
- **Assessment:** Completeness; nothing significant missed; professional English. **Gate to Phase 2.**

---

# PHASE 2 — Detecting Danger (Weeks 5–8)

*The cadet learns to find the threat in the picture: relative motion, CPA, TCPA, and when a situation legally becomes "risk of collision" (COLREGS Rules 6 & 7).*

### Week 5 — Relative Motion
- **STCW skill:** Collision assessment
- **Scenario:** One own-ship course/speed against moving traffic.
- **Task:** Determine whether each contact is opening or closing. Identify the difference between true and relative motion.
- **English deliverable:** A risk note stating which contacts are closing and require monitoring.
- **Assessment:** Closing contacts correctly identified.

### Week 6 — CPA and TCPA
- **STCW skill:** Collision assessment (CPA/TCPA)
- **Scenario:** Selected crossing contacts with defined courses/speeds.
- **Task:** Calculate (or read from ARPA-style tools) the Closest Point of Approach and Time to CPA for the closest contacts.
- **English deliverable:** A report stating CPA/TCPA values and what they mean for safety.
- **Assessment:** CPA/TCPA reasoning correct; cadet connects numbers to danger.

### Week 7 — Risk of Collision (Rule 7)
- **STCW skill:** Collision avoidance — risk determination
- **Scenario:** A developing crossing situation with a steady compass bearing.
- **Task:** Apply COLREGS Rule 7. Recognize that a steady bearing + decreasing range = risk of collision exists. Avoid "assuming" risk on scanty information.
- **English deliverable:** A reasoned statement of whether risk of collision exists and why, citing the steady-bearing principle.
- **Assessment:** Correct application of Rule 7 logic.

### Week 8 — Multi-Target Watch (Phase 2 checkpoint)
- **STCW skill:** Traffic monitoring under load
- **Scenario:** Dense Tampico picture, several simultaneous contacts.
- **Task:** Prioritize threats. Which contact is most dangerous and why? Manage attention across multiple targets.
- **English deliverable:** A prioritized threat brief.
- **Assessment:** Correct prioritization; reasoning sound. **Gate to Phase 3.**

---

# PHASE 3 — The Rules of the Road (Weeks 9–12)

*The cadet learns COLREGS application: who gives way, who stands on, and what action to take (Rules 8, 13–18). This is the legal and operational heart of watchkeeping.*

### Week 9 — Crossing Situations (Rule 15)
- **STCW skill:** Collision avoidance — COLREGS
- **Scenario:** Two power-driven vessels crossing.
- **Task:** Identify the give-way and stand-on vessel. State the required action of each.
- **English deliverable:** A decision report naming give-way/stand-on roles and the action.
- **Assessment:** Correct role assignment; correct action (Rule 16/17).

### Week 10 — Head-on and Overtaking (Rules 13 & 14)
- **STCW skill:** Collision avoidance — COLREGS
- **Scenario:** A head-on meeting and a separate overtaking situation.
- **Task:** Distinguish the two. Apply the correct rule to each (both alter to starboard in head-on; overtaking vessel keeps clear).
- **English deliverable:** A comparison report explaining the action for each situation.
- **Assessment:** Situations correctly distinguished and resolved.

### Week 11 — Responsibilities Between Vessels (Rule 18)
- **STCW skill:** Collision avoidance — vessel priority
- **Scenario:** Tampico traffic including a tug with tow, a vessel restricted in ability to manoeuvre, and a vessel constrained by draught.
- **Task:** Establish the hierarchy of responsibility. Who keeps out of whose way?
- **English deliverable:** A priority report applying Rule 18 to the real Tampico vessel mix.
- **Assessment:** Correct hierarchy; correct reading of vessel categories from AIS/lights/shapes.

### Week 12 — Restricted Visibility (Rule 19) (Phase 3 checkpoint)
- **STCW skill:** Collision avoidance in restricted visibility
- **Scenario:** Tampico picture + fog/heavy weather conditions (weather API integration — links to library "Heavy Weather" / "Severe Weather" units).
- **Task:** Apply Rule 19. No stand-on vessel in restricted visibility — every vessel navigates with caution. Determine safe action.
- **English deliverable:** A restricted-visibility action report, including sound signal awareness.
- **Assessment:** Correct Rule 19 application; safe speed reasoning. **Gate to Phase 4.**

---

# PHASE 4 — Bridge Communication (Weeks 13–16)

*The cadet learns to communicate the watch in standard maritime English: VHF, SMCP, VTS reporting, and bridge team coordination. Competency 7 made operational.*

### Week 13 — SMCP Fundamentals
- **STCW skill:** Reporting procedures; Maritime English
- **Scenario:** Routine bridge communications.
- **Task:** Use SMCP message markers correctly (Instruction, Advice, Warning, Information, Question, Answer, Request, Intention). Convert plain English into SMCP.
- **English deliverable:** A set of SMCP-formatted messages for routine situations.
- **Assessment:** Message markers used correctly; phrasing standard.

### Week 14 — VHF Bridge-to-Bridge
- **STCW skill:** Bridge communications
- **Scenario:** A crossing situation requiring VHF coordination with another vessel.
- **Task:** Conduct a bridge-to-bridge exchange to agree on passing arrangements. Phonetic spelling, clear call/response.
- **English deliverable:** A scripted (and recorded) VHF exchange in SMCP.
- **Assessment:** Protocol correct; intentions unambiguous; readback confirmed.

### Week 15 — VTS Reporting (Tampico Port Control)
- **STCW skill:** Reporting procedures
- **Scenario:** Vessel entering Tampico VTS area; live or captured traffic.
- **Task:** Make the required position/intention report to Vessel Traffic Service. Respond to VTS instructions.
- **English deliverable:** A complete VTS report (vessel identity, position, course, speed, intentions).
- **Assessment:** Report complete and correctly formatted; responds appropriately to VTS.

### Week 16 — Internal Bridge Team Communication (Phase 4 checkpoint)
- **STCW skill:** Bridge resource management; reporting
- **Scenario:** A developing traffic situation requiring orders to helm and reports to the Master.
- **Task:** Give helm/engine orders correctly. Report the situation to the Master using a clear, complete brief.
- **English deliverable:** Standard helm orders + a Master situation report.
- **Assessment:** Orders standard and correct; Master brief complete. **Gate to Phase 5.**

---

# PHASE 5 — The Full Watch (Weeks 17–18)

*Everything integrates. The cadet stands a complete watch and performs a professional handover.*

### Week 17 — Watch Handover
- **STCW skill:** Watch handover
- **Scenario:** Cadet relieves the watch in a live/captured Tampico situation.
- **Task:** Complete a full handover: traffic, navigation, conditions, standing orders, anything outstanding. Decide whether it is safe to accept the watch.
- **English deliverable:** A full verbal handover brief in SMCP, plus the decision to accept (or not) the watch.
- **Assessment:** Handover complete; correct decision on accepting the watch.

### Week 18 — Integrated Watch Assessment (Capstone)
- **STCW skill:** All Competency 2 skills integrated
- **Scenario:** A full dynamic watch — multiple contacts, a developing collision risk, changing weather, a required report — under time pressure.
- **Task:** Stand the watch. Read the picture, detect the danger, apply COLREGS, take and communicate action, report to the Master / VTS. End to end.
- **English deliverable:** All communications in SMCP; a final watch log entry.
- **Assessment:** Capstone rubric covering every phase. This is the semester's competency demonstration for OICNW Competency 2.

## Data & API Integration by Phase

| Phase | Data source | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| 1 | Captured scenario (`tampico-watch-01.json`) | No quota cost; stable picture |
| 2 | Captured scenarios + computed CPA/TCPA | Multiple captures show motion |
| 3 | Captured scenarios (vessel-type mix) | Real Tampico vessel variety |
| 4 | Captured + live AIS for realism | VTS area = Tampico port |
| 5 | Live AIS (AISstream) + weather/sea-state API | Full dynamic watch |

**Quota discipline:** student-facing tasks run on **captured scenarios** (zero ongoing quota). Live AIS is reserved for demonstration and the Phase 5 capstone, served through a **server-side cache** (one shared fetch, not one per student).

- Phase 2 weeks: **Standard**, ~25 XP each
- Phase 3 weeks: **Standard / Advanced**, ~30 XP each

## How This Scales to Other Semesters

This progression is the **Semester VII** (officer-level) build. The same five-phase spine scales down for earlier semesters by adjusting difficulty and competency depth:

- **Early semesters (I–III):** Phase 1–2 depth only — identification, basic awareness, simple reporting. Foundation difficulty.
- **Mid semesters (V):** Through Phase 3 — COLREGS introduced, supervised.
- **Senior semesters (VII):** Full 18-week build above, independent watch standing.

The data, scenarios, and SMCP backbone are shared; only the cognitive demand and autonomy change.
