S# STCW Competencies Reference

## Purpose

This document serves as the master reference for all competencies used throughout the SMCP Trainer Academy.

All missions, vocabulary, assessments, scenarios, and simulations must support one or more STCW competencies.

The goal is not simply to learn English.

The goal is to develop competent maritime officers capable of operating safely in international shipping environments.

---

# Officer in Charge of a Navigational Watch (OICNW)

Reference:

STCW Convention
Table A-II/1

---

## Competency 1

Plan and conduct a passage and determine position

Related Skills:

* Voyage planning
* Route monitoring
* Position fixing
* Navigation charts
* ECDIS operation
* GPS usage
* Radar navigation

---

## Competency 2

Maintain a safe navigational watch

Related Skills:

* Lookout duties
* Bridge watchkeeping
* Situational awareness
* Traffic monitoring
* Collision avoidance
* Reporting procedures
* Watch handover

---

## Competency 3

Use radar and ARPA to maintain safety of navigation

Related Skills:

* Radar interpretation
* Target tracking
* CPA
* TCPA
* Collision assessment
* Bridge decision making

---

## Competency 4

Use ECDIS to maintain safety of navigation

Related Skills:

* Route planning
* Route monitoring
* Chart updates
* Safety settings
* Alarm management

---

## Competency 5

Respond to emergencies

Related Skills:

* Fire response
* Man overboard
* Abandon ship
* Emergency communications
* Muster procedures

---

## Competency 6

Respond to distress signals at sea

Related Skills:

* GMDSS awareness
* Distress communications
* Search and rescue support

---

## Competency 7

Use English in written and oral form

Related Skills:

* Maritime English
* SMCP
* Bridge communications
* Reporting
* Logbook entries
* Safety communications

---

## Competency 8

Transmit and receive information by visual signaling

Related Skills:

* Flags
* Lights
* Morse code awareness

---

# Academy Design Rule

Every mission must support at least one STCW competency.

Every vocabulary lesson must support at least one STCW competency.

Every assessment must measure one or more STCW competencies.

No content should exist without a connection to STCW requirements.
