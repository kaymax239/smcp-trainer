# Course 02: Basic SMCP Communications

School:
KAYMAX Maritime Academy

Level:
Officer Cadet Foundation

Course Type:
Beginner SMCP Course

---

## Course Objective

This course teaches maritime cadets how to use simple Standard Marine Communication Phrases during normal shipboard operations.

Cadets will learn how to give short reports, confirm orders, ask for repetition, and communicate clearly with deck officers, engine officers, and other crew members.

The goal is clear and safe maritime English.

---

## Target Students

This course is for:

- Deck cadets
- Engine cadets
- New maritime students
- Beginner Maritime English learners
- Cadets preparing for bridge or engine-room communication

Students should know basic ship vocabulary before starting this course.

## Deck Officer Example

Situation:
The Officer of the Watch gives an order to a deck cadet.

Officer:
Proceed to the forecastle and check the mooring lines.

Cadet:
Order confirmed. I will proceed to the forecastle and check the mooring lines.

Officer:
Report status when finished.

Cadet:
I understand. I will report status when finished.

Cadet Report:
Mooring lines checked. Status is normal.

---

## Engine Officer Example

Situation:
The Second Engineer gives an instruction to an engine cadet.

Second Engineer:
Proceed to the engine room and check the cooling water temperature.

Cadet:
Order confirmed. I will proceed to the engine room and check the cooling water temperature.

Second Engineer:
Report any abnormal reading.

Cadet:
I understand. I will report any abnormal reading.

Cadet Report:
Cooling water temperature checked. Status is normal.

# Lesson 01: Clear Maritime Communication

## Objective

Understand why maritime communication must be short, clear, and confirmed.

## Key Points

- Use simple words.
- Speak slowly.
- Confirm important orders.
- Ask for repetition when needed.
- Do not guess.

## Practice Phrases

- Say again.
- Please repeat.
- I understand.
- I do not understand.
- Order confirmed.

## Simple Practice

Officer:
Check the safety equipment.

Cadet:
Order confirmed. I will check the safety equipment.

---

# Lesson 02: Reporting Status

## Objective

Learn how to report normal and abnormal conditions.

## Key Points

- A status report tells the officer what is happening.
- A report must be short and accurate.
- Report unsafe conditions immediately.

## Practice Phrases

- Report status.
- Status is normal.
- Status is not normal.
- Equipment checked.
- I require assistance.

## Deck Example

Cadet:
Lifebuoy checked. Status is normal.

## Engine Example

Cadet:
Pump pressure checked. Status is not normal. I require assistance.

---

# Lesson 03: Confirming Orders

## Objective

Practice closed-loop communication.

## Key Points

- Listen to the order.
- Repeat the important action.
- Confirm the order.
- Complete the task.
- Report back.

## Practice Phrases

- Confirm order.
- Order confirmed.
- Standing by.
- Task completed.
- Reporting back.

## Simple Practice

Officer:
Stand by on channel 16.

Cadet:
Standing by on channel 16. Order confirmed.

---

# Lesson 04: Asking for Repetition and Clarification

## Objective

Learn what to say when a message is not clear.

## Key Points

- It is safe to ask for repetition.
- Never pretend to understand.
- Use standard phrases.

## Practice Phrases

- Say again.
- Please repeat.
- I do not understand.
- Please confirm.
- Message received.

## Simple Practice

Officer:
Proceed to the aft station.

Cadet:
Please repeat.

Officer:
Proceed to the aft station.

Cadet:
Message received. I will proceed to the aft station.

---

# Lesson 05: Basic Emergency Communication

## Objective

Use simple SMCP phrases during emergency situations.

## Key Points

- Speak clearly.
- Report the emergency location.
- Report the emergency type.
- Request assistance if needed.

## Practice Phrases

- Fire reported.
- Man overboard.
- Emergency alarm activated.
- I require assistance.
- Stop operation.
- Keep clear.

## Deck Example

Cadet:
Man overboard on starboard side. I require assistance.

## Engine Example

Cadet:
Fire reported in engine room. Emergency alarm activated.

## Role-Play Activity 01: Deck Safety Check

Roles:

- Officer of the Watch
- Deck Cadet

Task:

The officer orders the cadet to check deck safety equipment.

Required phrases:

- Order confirmed.
- Equipment checked.
- Status is normal.
- I require assistance.

Success Criteria:

- The cadet confirms the order.
- The cadet reports the result clearly.
- The cadet uses simple maritime English.

---

## Role-Play Activity 02: Engine Room Status Report

Roles:

- Second Engineer
- Engine Cadet

Task:

The engineer orders the cadet to check temperature and pressure readings.

Required phrases:

- Proceed to the engine room.
- Order confirmed.
- Report status.
- Status is normal.
- Status is not normal.

Success Criteria:

- The cadet repeats the task correctly.
- The cadet reports one normal reading.
- The cadet reports one abnormal reading.

---

## Role-Play Activity 03: Message Not Understood

Roles:

- Chief Officer
- Cadet

Task:

The officer gives an unclear instruction. The cadet must ask for repetition and confirm the final order.

Required phrases:

- Say again.
- Please repeat.
- I do not understand.
- Message received.
- Order confirmed.

Success Criteria:

- The cadet does not guess.
- The cadet asks for repetition.
- The cadet confirms the correct instruction.

## Assessment: Basic SMCP Communication Check

Assessment Type:
Oral and written practice

Passing Score:
80 percent

1. bridge
2. engine room
3. confirm
4. report
5. emergency

## Part 2: Phrase Completion

Complete the phrases:

1. Say _____.
2. Order _____.
3. Status is _____.
4. I require _____.

## Part 3: Role-Play Test

The instructor gives the cadet three simple orders.

The cadet must:

- Repeat the order.
- Confirm the order.
- Give a short status report.
- Ask for repetition if the message is not clear.

## Part 4: Emergency Report

The cadet must give one deck emergency report and one engine-room emergency report.

Deck example:
Man overboard on port side. I require assistance.

Engine example:
Fire reported in engine room. Emergency alarm activated.

## Completion Standard

The cadet completes the course when they can:

- Use basic SMCP phrases clearly.
- Confirm simple orders.
- Report normal and abnormal status.
- Ask for repetition.
- Give a simple emergency report.
